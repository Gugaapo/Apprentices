# The Time Loop

> - **What it is:** Every month, time resets. People who retain their memories are called **loopers**, distinguished by a marker on their souls.
> - **How it works:** No actual time travel — a "blueprint" of the material plane is copied into a pocket dimension, run for one month, then destroyed. Only marked souls persist.
> - **Why it matters:** This is the campaign's core mechanic — everything the players learn, build, and plan exists within this framework.

## How It Works

[[40_People/NPCs/Allies/Zach Noveda\|Zach]] was originally the only looper. [[40_People/NPCs/Enemies/Quatach-Ichl\|Quatach-Ichl]] used **soul meld** on him and the players, transferring a chunk of Zach's soul (along with the marker) to them. This caused them to loop as well.

The [[20_World/02 The Imperial Artifacts\|Imperial Crown]] allows the Controller to place a **temporary marker** (referred to as a "lesser marker" by the [[40_People/NPCs/Neutral/Guardian of the Threshold\|Guardian of the Threshold]]), which grants the marked person persistence for **six restarts**. Such people are called **temporary loopers**.

## Key Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What persists** | Memories, spellcasting skills, mana reserves |
| **What resets** | Physical body — injuries AND improvements are removed each month |
| **Loop end triggers** | Zach's death, or the emergence of a primordial like [[40_People/NPCs/Enemies/Panaxeth\|Panaxeth]] |
| **Marker tracking** | The soul marker can be tracked and has a switch to end the iteration |

## The Simulation

No actual time travel takes place. A "blueprint" of the material plane is taken. For each iteration, a copy is produced inside a pocket dimension, allowed to exist for up to one month, then destroyed — except for the marked souls.
