## Description

The Kingdom of Eldemar is the primary national setting of this campaign. It is one of the "Big Three" major powers of the [[30_Locations/Continents/Altazia\|Altazia]] continent, originally the head of a group of loosely allied states called the **Old Alliance** (officially the Alliance of Eldemar).

A terrible king and internal problems caused the alliance to fracture. The subsequent **[[20_World/History/Timeline/06_The_Splinter_Wars\|Splinter Wars]]** — made especially devastating by the introduction of firearms, which allowed smaller nations to defeat those with powerful mage armies — split it apart further. Today, Eldemar is formed from the core of the Old Alliance. It is ruled by a King, though the **Council of Elders** (composed of [[40_People/Factions/Houses/Noble_Houses_Overview\|Noble Houses]]) holds immense political sway.

## Geography and Cities
Eldemar is strategically located in [[30_Locations/Regions/Altazias Central Valley\|Altazia's Central Valley]]. Its major settlements include:
- **[[30_Locations/Cities and Towns/Cyoria\|Cyoria]]**: The intellectual heart of the kingdom, home to the Royal Academy of Magical Arts.
- **[[30_Locations/Cities and Towns/Korsa\|Korsa]]**: The third-largest city and a major industrial trade hub.
- **[[30_Locations/Cities and Towns/Knyazov Dveri\|Knyazov Dveri]]**: A northern town known as the "Prince's Gates."
- **[[30_Locations/Cities and Towns/Cirin\|Cirin]]**: A smaller town and birthplace of the party.

---

## Modern State

Today, Eldemar is a **smaller but fiercely nationalistic** monarchy that yearns for its former glory and refuses to accept that the alliance is over. The kingdom is currently engaged in the **Great Northern Push**, an effort to colonize the northern wilderness.

The kingdom is also undergoing a **[[20_World/Society/Technological Revolution\|Technological Revolution]]**, integrating trains and mass-produced firearms. It is leading the charge in the **[[20_World/Society/Spread of Magical Training\|democratization of magic]]**, allowing commoners to attend prestigious academies.
