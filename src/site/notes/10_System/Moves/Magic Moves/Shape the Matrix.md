# Shape the Matrix

*A Magic Move. Available to all Casters.*

When you **project or trace a geometric formula to manipulate reality**, first declare your Function and Medium and state your fictional intent. If you have the structural mastery, choose the **Tags** you are attempting to impart (e.g., *Forceful, Messy, Restraining, Defensive, Area*).

Then, roll:
- **+Finesse** (for precise, delicate, or defensive shaping)
- **+Willpower** (for brute force, destructive, or desperate channeling)

- **10+ (Strong Hit):** The formula perfectly locks into reality. You achieve your intent cleanly. Choose one:
  - The spell gains an additional beneficial Tag (e.g., *Lingering, Piercing*).
  - You recover or retain narrative momentum (gain a +1 Forward).
  - You cast it silently or cleanly enough to absolutely guarantee no collateral damage or unwanted attention.
- **7-9 (Mixed Success):** You successfully cast the spell and achieve your intent, but the matrix strains you or the environment. The GM chooses one consequence from the **Matrix Collapse Menu**:
  - **Neural Strain (Harm):** Take **1-Wound** immediately (ignores armor/wards) due to mana friction. Take 2-Wounds if the spell was highly ambitious.
  - **Structural Bleed (Collateral):** The magic bleeds into the environment in unintended, dangerous ways.
  - **Resonant Echo:** It attracts immediate, dangerous attention from enemies or beasts.
  - **Bleeding Medium:** The element remains as a persistent environmental hazard (smoke, mud, residual static).
  - **Geometric Blindness:** You are locked out of using that specific Function or Medium for the rest of the scene.
  - **The Monkey's Paw:** The spell interprets your intent in the most unhelpful literal way possible.
- **6- (Miss):** The matrix collapses instantly. The spell fails, mutates against you, or causes severe backlash. The GM makes a Hard Move.
