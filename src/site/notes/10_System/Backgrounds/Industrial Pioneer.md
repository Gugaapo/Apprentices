# Industrial Pioneer

*While the nobles cling to their ancient magical traditions, you have your hands in the grease and soot of Eldemar's future. The world is changing. Trains, firearms, and mass-produced alchemy are democratizing power, and you are part of that revolution.*

## Overview

Your family isn't composed of high mages or politicians; they are engineers, factory floor managers, or locomotive conductors. You were raised around the rhythmic pounding of steam engines and the smell of industrial chemicals. When your magical talent was discovered, you didn't view it as a mystical calling, but rather as a highly lucrative tool for engineering and industry.

You approach magic with a practical, scientific mindset. Where others see a majestic firewall, you see an inefficient combustion reaction. You are comfortable in the gritty industrial sectors of cities like Korsa and Cyoria, and you understand the machines that the old aristocratic mages look down upon.

---

## Fictional Permission
You intuitively grasp the basic functions and weak points of physical clockwork and steam machinery.

## Mechanical Benefits

**Background Move — Mechanist:** When you roll `+Mental` to manipulate, repair, or break mundane clockwork/industrial machinery, take +1 [[10_System/Glossary/Forward\|Forward]].

## Connection (Anchor)
**Connection — Factory Foreman:** You have ties to the industrial and manufacturing sectors. once per quest, you can leverage your familial or personal connections to acquire industrial-grade parts, specialized tools, black-powder explosives, or access to heavy machinery and workshops outside of the Academy.

---

## Roleplay Hooks
*   **The Tinker:** You are constantly disassembling and reassembling small gadgets, integrating minor spell-formulas into mechanical devices.
*   **New Money:** You are deeply annoyed by the traditionalism of the noble houses and actively support the democratization of magic.
*   **Industrial Accident:** You have a prominent scar or a prosthetic from a factory accident in your youth, serving as a reminder of the dangers of mundane technology.
