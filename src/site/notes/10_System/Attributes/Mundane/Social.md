# Social

Your **Social** attribute dictates your charm, deception, intuition regarding people's intentions, and your ability to leverage personal connections. In a world ruled by Noble Houses, guild hierarchies, and clandestine syndicates, being able to read a room is often more vital to survival than throwing a fireball. 

## Mechanical Uses

Whenever your character attempts to influence an NPC or deduce their motivations using soft skills, you add your **Social** rating to the 2d6 roll.

### Associated Basic Moves
- **[[10_System/Moves/01 Basic Moves#Manipulate and Provoke\|Manipulate and Provoke]]:** Used when you have leverage and use it to charm, bribe, threaten, or extort an NPC into doing what you want.

### Information and Economy
A high Social attribute is incredibly powerful for tracking down black market goods, discovering secrets, and gathering rumors. When attempting to earn [[10_System/02 Economy\|Funds]] through networking, scamming, or leveraging contacts, the GM may ask for a Social roll.
