> **Purpose:** Magic is the physical act of shaping geometric structures in your soul and forcing raw atmospheric mana through them. This act is called **[[10_System/Glossary/Shaping\|Shaping]]**. This document teaches how to structure intents, use your vocabulary, and resolve spells in combat using a fiction-first narrative engine.

---

## 1. Core Loop & Resolution

Magic operates natively via the **Magic Moves** (see [[10_System/01 Core Mechanics\|01 Core Mechanics]]). The Core engine utilizes the standard PbtA 2d6 engine (6-: Miss, 7-9: Mixed Success, 10+: Strong Hit).

There are two primary axes of magic:
- **The Fictional Positioning:** Does your character know the requisite Glyphs, and does your proposed geometric structure physically make sense in the immediate fiction? An apprentice cannot burn down a city block with a simple glyph sequence, regardless of how well they roll.
- **The Roll:** When you try to alter reality with your 3D matrices, you trigger the **Shape the Matrix** Move.

### The Move
If you have the appropriate glyphs, you trigger **Shape the Matrix**.
*   **Precision/Defensive:** Roll `2d6 + Finesse`
*   **Destructive/Raw Force:** Roll `2d6 + Willpower`

*Note: You do not use Target Numbers (TNs), nor are there abstract "Mana Pools". A 10+ is an objective success. A 7-9 means you succeed but suffer narrative complications or explicit Harm due to the soul strain.*

## 2. The Language of Magic (The Geometric Index)

Students do not abstractly "learn a firebolt spell." By studying, they unlock spatial 3D structures that enable them to construct mathematical **Matrices**. Matrices are the geometric blueprints modeled in your mind; a **Spell** is the physical manifestation of those structures in reality.

Every matrix is built with **Glyphs**:
1. **Exactly ONE Function (Core Geometry):** The core School pushing the magic (e.g., `Schf` / Create).
2. **Exactly ONE Medium (Material Glyph):** The physical element being manipulated (e.g., `Feur` / Fire).
3. **Optional Modifiers:** Extra tags shaping trajectory, scale, or duration (e.g., `Kupp` / Dome).

### The Tri-Tier Progression System
Glyphs are fundamentally categorized into three tiers of learning. **Progression is driven by in-game discovery, not simply spending points.**

1. **The Core Index (The Base Forms)**
   - **How to acquire:** Purchasing 1 Dot in a **Core School** (e.g., Projection, Alteration) automatically grants the mage structural mastery in that school's standard `Functions` and base `Mediums`. No individual glyph tracking is required.

2. **The Advanced Index (Specialized Geometry)**
   - **How to acquire:** **Specializations** (e.g., Combat Magic) are advanced structural blueprints. You do not simply buy modifiers in a void; you must uncover them. You might learn 3 Advanced Modifiers by studying under a battle-hardened Archmage or stealing a military academy's curriculum. 

3. **The Wild Index (The Loot)**
   - **How to acquire:** Extreme scale modifiers (like `Gros` / Greater) or terrifying nouns (like `Plaz` / Plasma) are not taught at the Academy. They are exclusively considered **Wild Glyphs** and can only be discovered physically in the world through adventuring—such as dissecting the soul-matrix of a Lich, or interpreting the scrawlings in a forgotten ruin.

## 3. Casting Procedure

Every spell creation follows strict steps: **Declare Intent, Validate Positioning, and Roll.**

### Step 1: Declare Intent & Fictional Positioning
The player states their ultimate intent and the sequence (`Function + Medium`) they are using. The GM determines if the character has the **fictional permission** to achieve this.
- *Example:* An Apprentice wanting to light a campfire with `Schf + Feur` has clear fictional permission. An Apprentice wanting to incinerate a charging war-golem with the identical structure *does not*. The GM will simply say, "You don't have the magical knowledge or the Wild Glyphs to fuel something that massive. What do you actually do?"

By discovering more Glyphs (finding Advanced and Wild modifiers), you give yourself the fictional permission to achieve grander intents. 

### Step 2: Choose Tags
Instead of counting damage numbers or range increments, declare the **Tags** you want the spell to carry based on the modifiers you weave into it (e.g., *Forceful, Messy, Area, Restraining, Lingering*). 
- *Pushing the Matrix:* If you string too many wild modifiers together or attempt a structure far beyond your current tier's capacity, the GM may warn you: *"That's highly unstable geometry. If you cast that, you must take 1-Wound of strain before you even roll."*

### Step 3: Roll and Resolve
Roll **Shape the Matrix** (`+Finesse` or `+Willpower`). Choose your options on a 10+, or brace for a Matrix Collapse on a 7-9.

## 4. Advanced Magical Friction & Matrix Collapses

There is no "Mana Burn" math. Failing to structure geometry perfectly causes immediate physical or narrative consequences. If you roll a 7-9 on **Shape the Matrix**, the GM will inflict one of the following collapses upon you:

### The Matrix Collapse Menu
1. **Neural Strain (Harm):** Pushing mana strains your organic channels. Take **1-Wound** immediately (ignores armor/wards). If you were casting something catastrophically large, take **2-Wounds**.
2. **Structural Bleed (Collateral):** The magic bleeds into the environment. Fictional collateral happens—you blind an ally, ignite the priceless tapestry, or warp the floorboards you're standing on.
3. **Resonant Echo (Attention):** Your geometry was aggressively loud or glowed with terrifying brightness. You attract immediate, dangerous attention from lingering threats or anti-mage forces.
4. **Bleeding Medium:** The element you summoned sticks around as an ongoing environmental hazard. The *Earth* spell leaves the room choked in deep *Mud*; the *Fire* leaves suffocating *Smoke*, creating an immediate obstacle.
5. **Geometric Blindness:** The specific `Function` or `Medium` spatial structure you just used is "burned out" of your mind. You cannot use it again until you take a beat to let your neuro-pathways reset.
6. **The Monkey's Paw:** The spell achieves *exactly* what you asked for, but in the most unhelpful literal biological or physical interpretation possible.

### Wards and Reactions
Targets are not passive. If an enemy shoots magic at you, you may use a Mastered defensive spell as an instant Reaction using the **Deflect Magic** Move, but relying too heavily on desperate reactions will quickly accumulate strain.

## APPENDIX: Quick Casting Card

### 1. BUILD THE MATRIX
Syntax: `[FUNCTION] + [MEDIUM] + [TAGS (Modifiers)]`
*Example: Lebe (Animate) + Waff (Weapon) + [Restraining]*

### 2. CHECK FICTIONAL POSITIONING
- Does your character know these glyphs?
- Does your geometric index logically match the scale of the intent? 
- *If pushing the limit: Accept pre-emptive Harm or a GM complication.*

### 3. ROLL THE DICE
- Roll **Shape the Matrix** Move.
- `2d6 + Finesse` or `Willpower`

### 4. RESOLVE
- **10+:** Flawless matrix! Achieve your intent and choose a benefit (Extra Tag, +1 Forward, No Collateral).
- **7-9:** The spell manifests, but triggers the **Matrix Collapse Menu** (Take Harm, Cause Collateral, Attract Attention, etc.).
- **6-:** Total matrix collapse. The GM makes a Hard Move against you.
