# Combat Magic

**Classification:** Specialization
**Parent School:** [[10_System/Schools/Projection/Projection\|Projection]]

# Lore
Combat Magic is not a standalone discipline — it is a category of practice and casting philosophy applied to spells from multiple schools. It refers to spells optimized for battle: fast casting time, high power output, designed to overcome defenses with minimal preparation. Magic is now cast by weaving geometric roots (Max 4 letters) into compound spell names.
> **Casting Roll:** 2d6 + Willpower + [Projection]

# Lore deep dive

> [!NOTE] Multi-School
> Combat Magic draws primarily from **[[10_System/Schools/Projection/Projection\|Projection]], [[10_System/Schools/Abjuration/Negation\|Negation]], [[10_System/Schools/Animation/Animation\|Animation]], and [[10_System/Schools/Dimensionalism/Conjuration\|Conjuration]]**, followed by [[10_System/Schools/Alteration/Alteration\|Alteration]] and [[10_System/Schools/Dimensionalism/Dimensionalism\|Dimensionalism]]. It is not something you learn as a separate skill — it is a *way of learning and casting* that emphasises speed and power over elegance and safety.

> [!WARNING] Cost & Risk
> Combat spells place extreme demands on the mage's Capacity. Getting the spell wrong is more likely to cause severe Mana Burn than normal magic. Combat magic prioritises power over mana efficiency and safety features.

Combat magic is probably the most well-known and popular field of study that draws upon multiple magical disciplines. It is **deceptively simple**. 

On one hand, most combat spells have rather modest shaping requirements and can be learned quite quickly by a dedicated mage. On the other hand, the very traits that make these spells so potent in combat inevitably come at the cost of mana efficiency and safety features. Getting a combat spell wrong is highly likely to hurt the caster. This, along with the inherently lethal nature of fighting for a living, means that combat magic is considered quite hard to truly master. Many mages know how to cast a combat spell or two for emergencies, but they would not dare call themselves combat mages.

**The Economic and Political Reality:** Despite its dangers, combat magic is very common everywhere, and is incredibly popular as a specialty. Even people who are not naturally suited for it often direct their energies into the field. Most states enthusiastically encourage their citizens to focus on this discipline, as there is always a high demand for more battlemages everywhere.

## Mechanical Focus
Combat casting uses [[10_System/Attributes/Magical/Willpower\|Willpower]] for raw output and [[10_System/Attributes/Magical/Finesse\|Finesse]] for targeting. A **Combat Magic** specialty allows the player to reduce the casting time of known spell formulas by weaving in their exclusive fast-cast glyphs.
