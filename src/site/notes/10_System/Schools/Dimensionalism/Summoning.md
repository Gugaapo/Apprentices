# Summoning (External)

*Summing is a sub-field of Dimensionalism dealing specifically with the retrieval and binding of entities, familiars, creatures, objects, and (at extreme tiers) spirits from other planes.*
**Classification:** Specialization **Parent School:** [[10_System/Schools/Dimensionalism/Dimensionalism\|Dimensionalism]]

## Fictional Permission
When you hold `Summoning` in your Grimoire, you have specific fictional positioning.

**Summoning CAN:**
- Pull an explicitly marked object to the caster's hand.
- Transport a bonded familiar to the caster's location (`Ruff`, `Wese`).
- Bind summoned magical beasts to the caster's will (temporarily).

**Summoning CANNOT:**
- Summon a person or object that does not have a pre-established magical anchor or soul bond.
- Be used cheaply. Maintaining anchors drains passive mana reserves constantly.

## Geometric Scaling (Mana Track Box Costs)
When determining the cost of a spell, the GM uses this scale:
- **Trivial (0 Ticks):** Summons a tiny, harmless insect from nearby. (`Ruff Klen Wese`)
- **Regular (1 Tick):** Pulls a small animal like a dog or bird to the caster. (`Ruff Wese`)
- **Hard (2 Ticks):** Summons a specific beast from miles away. (`Ruff Wese Fern`)
- **Complex (2 Ticks):** Tears open a portal to pull a dangerous monster through. (`Tohr Ruff Wese`)
- **Miracle (4+ Ticks):** Summons an ancient beast from the deep wilderness directly into the room. (`Ruff Gros Wese`)
