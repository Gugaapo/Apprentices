# Divination

**Classification:** Core School

Divination is the art of gathering and organizing information based on the caster's query. Scrying, magical sight, mapping spells, tracking spells, forensic magic, and magic designed to analyze things all fall under this discipline.

## [[50_Magic/Glossary/Specializations\|Specializations]]
As mages progress their dots in this School, they may elect to unlock highly advanced **[[50_Magic/Glossary/Specializations\|Specializations]]** by meeting secondary prerequisites. The specializations for this school are:
- **[[10_System/Schools/Divination/Scrying\|Scrying]]**
- **[[10_System/Schools/Divination/Chronomancy\|Chronomancy]]**

## Progression
Under Academy rules, acquiring a dot in a [[10_System/06 The Magic System#The Casting System\|School Proficiency]] does not instantly grant knowledge of every spell in that tier. Instead, purchasing a dot in Divination grants the mage **exactly one Spell (Power)** from this School (at that dot level or lower).

## Mechanical Focus
Divination provides information and bonuses to [[50_Magic/Glossary/Mind Defense\|Mind Defense]]. It relies heavily on [[10_System/Attributes/Memory\|Memory]] for processing data and [[10_System/Attributes/Finesse\|Finesse]] for shaping the spell cleanly.

## Lore Deep Dive

**Links and Limitations:** Divinations require some kind of link to the target, either a physical object or some of the caster’s memories. They are strictly range-limited—a caster cannot cast a divination on a target miles away without a powerful scrying focus. Spells seeking a non-specific target will only scan a certain area around the caster before giving up. Furthermore, divinations that aim to predict the future or reconstruct the past are only *guesses* based on the clues the spell was presented with. They tend to be unreliable, with future prediction being far more unreliable than past reconstruction.

**Efficiency and Skill:** Divination is not a very mana-intensive field. It puts a far greater emphasis on good shaping skills than the size of one’s mana reserves, making most divination spells rather cheap for their level. This makes the school a popular choice amongst mages with average or below-average mana reserves. 

**The 'Garbage In, Garbage Out' Syndrome:** Being able to cast a divination spell is no guarantee that it will be useful to you. Interpreting the information received can be fiendishly difficult, often resulting in a string of gibberish and a raging headache. If you feed improper information into your divination spell, or start with an ill-considered reference point, you will get a result that is incorrect or misleading.

**The Cost of Dedication:** Because of the sheer mental discipline required to interpret results correctly, Divination requires an uncommon amount of dedication. Most diviners are narrowly specialized, neglecting other fields of magic to focus entirely on their chosen discipline. Only the most talented diviners, or very old ones who have hit the point of diminishing returns in their studies, can afford to invest their energies into other fields.

## Design Boundaries

**Divination CAN:**
- Detect the presence, school, and tier of active magic within range
- Extend sensory perception through barriers (walls, floors, darkness)
- Read residual mana imprints left by past events (psychometry)
- View distant locations in real-time ([[10_System/Schools/Divination/Scrying\|Scrying]])
- Receive probabilistic impressions of future events (precognition, at high tiers)

**Divination CANNOT:**
- Apply any effect to the target of observation (no damage, no debuffs)
- Create false perceptions ([[10_System/Schools/Illusionism/Illusionism\|Illusionism]])
- Read minds (that requires [[10_System/Schools/Illusionism/Mind Magic\|Mind Magic]] via Illusionism + [[50_Magic/Traits/Merits/Empath\|Psychic Potential]])
- Reconstruct the past or future with 100% infallible accuracy (it is always an educated guess by the magic)

**The key verb is PERCEIVE.** If the spell's primary action is learning something the caster doesn't currently know, without changing anything, it belongs in Divination.
