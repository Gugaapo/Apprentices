# Artifice

Artifice is the practice of physically carving geometric shapes and Glyphs (Functions, Objects, Characteristics) into an object to limit [[10_System/Glossary/mana\|mana]] flow along pre-determined pathways. They anchor spells into a carefully-crafted physical diagram to make them persist indefinitely, forming the basis of permanent wards and magical items (e.g., engraved wands, rings, or enchanted bullets).

> [!NOTE] Mechanical Hook: Prepared Magic
> Players with dots in **Artifice** can inscribe their magic into items. Because the geometric math is eternally solved by the physical carving, the caster does not need to mentally load the structure. 
> 
> **1. Instant Activation:** Firing an Artifice ignores the caster's Calculation limit. It activates instantly—often as a reaction or free action.
> **2. No Rolling Required:** Using an Artifice requires **no 2d6 casting roll** (it represents automatic baseline success).
> **3. Double Mana Cost (The Built-In Limit):** Pushing raw mana passively through a physical object is wildly inefficient. Firing an Artifice instantly causes **Double the normal Mana cost** for those spell roots. This heavy inefficiency tax is the hard limit on the system: while a player can prepare 10 loaded wands during downtime, they mathematically cannot spam them endlessly without burning out their own aura.

## Mechanical Focus

Designing and inscribing an Artifice is a slow, meticulous process. Rolls to craft or modify them typically use **`2d6 + Calculation (or Finesse) + Artifice`** (representing mathematical expertise and magic theory). 

Creating an Artifice requires:
1. **The Schematic**: Knowledge of the specific Formula (e.g., *Schf Feur*).
2. **The Anchor**: A physical item able to withstand the mana channeled through it.
3. **The Root Words**: The crafter **must possess the required Root Words in their Grimoire** to physically engrave them into the item.

Once etched, the engraved roots are rigid; the spell **cannot be swapped, altered, or modulated**. A *Schf Feur* wand will only ever shoot standard fireballs.

## Lore Deep Dive

Artifice is notoriously difficult. The anchor must be able to withstand the mana being channeled through it, and the physical material it is carved into should not disrupt the spell boundary. This frequently necessitates the use of highly expensive, exotic materials in anchor construction.

You are taking the ephemeral geometry of a spoken spell and making it eternal. A failed roll means the carved geometry was a millimeter off; when you push mana through it, the "math" doesn't add up, causing it to violently shatter.

**Copying vs. Creating:** Even simply copying an existing geometry can be challenging. It requires high manual dexterity, immense patience, and the ability to carve all of the necessary glyph roots flawlessly into the final product. However, if a mage wants to actually *modify* the diagram even slightly, the difficulty skyrockets—requiring a massive amount of theoretical knowledge and mathematical expertise to pull off without the formula exploding or fizzling.

**The Economic Reality:** Because of this extreme difficulty, true Artifice experts tend to be very rare and extremely well paid. However, because the high demand for enchanted items makes their services too expensive for the average person, a massive number of mages end up "dabbling" in the field out of sheer necessity.
