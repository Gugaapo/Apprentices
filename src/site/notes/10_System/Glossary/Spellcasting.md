# Spellcasting

The mysterious substance of [[10_System/Glossary/mana\|mana]] is the foundation of magic, but mana does very little on its own. Something needs to take control of the mana and direct it towards a coherent goal. This directing, this *shaping* of mana, is the process of weaving geometric **Glyphs** into phrases to form "Spell Matrices", and physically pushing mana through spiritual canals until the effect manifests or the body's Capacity is reached.

## Prerequisite Skills

Before a person can perform magic, they must master five absolutely necessary skills:
1. Sense their own [[10_System/Glossary/mana\|mana]]
2. Quickly and reliably force their personal mana into specific geometric structures
3. Comprehend and memorize the Glyph roots (Functions, Objects, Characteristics)
4. Visualize the desired Formula with a high degree of detail and clarity
5. Exercise discipline to endure the physical friction (Mana Burn) of casting

Developing these into a seamless whole takes 2-4 years for an average mage. In modern times, specialized magic instructors and better training methods have vastly improved the success rate compared to historical apprenticeships. 

## Unstructured Magic (Tier 0)

The oldest and simplest form of magic is unstructured magic. The caster mentally assembles glyphs on the fly in combat without an established, memorized formula. 

*   **The Cost of Sloppiness:** Because the canals are sloppy and volatile when woven on the fly, doing this imposes a universal **+2 Difficulty (TN) to the casting roll** and costs **+1 additional Mana**, regardless of the success.
*   **Intent-First Scaling:** Calculating the exact complexity of a multi-syllable, cross-school unstructured spell in the middle of a battle takes too long. Therefore, when a player casts Unstructured Magic in combat, the GM doesn't calculate the cost based on the words. Instead, the player simply declares how much Mana they are willing to burn (e.g., "I'm pushing 2 Mana into this"). They then speak the phrase. The spell's effect and damage are rigidly handcuffed to the Mana spent. If they say a deeply complex, 5-glyph phrase but only spend 1 Mana, the spell still goes off—but it's hilariously weak, manifesting exactly at the "Regular" scale. 
*   **Flexibility:** Unstructured magic is incredibly flexible. The caster can adjust the details from moment to moment, adapting to changing circumstances agilely. Many structured magic defenses have trouble countering unstructured magic because it can adjust on the fly to attack weak points.

Every mage has some ability in unstructured magic, as it serves as a vital foundation for structured magic, but it is highly inefficient for complex combat.

## Structured Magic (Formulas)

Structured magic (also known as bounded magic or the divine limiter system) is a method of directing [[10_System/Glossary/mana\|mana]] in precise, forceful ways using rigid, pre-calculated mathematical formulas.

Spells are constructed out of **Glyphs** of at most 4 letters. By combining a Verb (e.g., `Schf` for Create) and a Noun (e.g., `Feur` for Fire), the mage forms a **Formula** (e.g., *Schf Feur*).

*   **Canal Friction:** The human soul is not a machine gun; pushing the exact same geometric shape through your mana canals repeatedly causes "Canal Friction." If a caster uses the same primary Function/Characteristic combination twice in the same scene, the Target Number (Difficulty) increases by +2 for the second cast, and +4 for the third.
*   **Proxies (Chants and Gestures):** To cast a structured spell efficiently, a student must spend downtime officially structuring the geometric math as a "Formula" in their notes. Casting these unmastered formulas requires chanting the phrase and tracing the geometry with their hands.
*   **Language:** Modern mages typically use these specific 4-letter roots. Spell elements can be bound to new words and gestures (crucial for non-human species like [[70_Bestiary/Sapient_Species/Aranea\|Aranea]]), but translating the entire spellcasting language is a massive undertaking.
*   **Limitations:** Structured spells are rigid. The magic mostly does its own thing once cast, leaving the caster with limited ability to change its behavior. 
*   **Mastery (Muscle Memory):** If a mage spends Study Points to "Master" a specific combination of Glyphs, they commit the geometry to pure muscle memory. This permanently lowers its Complexity Category by 1 step (reducing the Mana cost) and allows the spell to be cast silently and instantly, without vocal or somatic proxies.
