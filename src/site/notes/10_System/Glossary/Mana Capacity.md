**Mana Capacity** represents the maximum amount of magical energy a character's body and spiritual canals can safely process before tearing themselves apart. It is a hard **Mana Capacity**, not an abstract pool of points.

**Calculation:** `4 + Core Resonance`

This limit is vital for spellcasting. Every structured spell costs an amount of Mana directly equal to its Complexity (e.g., a *Hard* spell costs 2 Mana). As you cast spells, you accumulate this Mana friction. 

If the total spent Mana exceeds your Capacity, your body begins to break down, plunging you into the **Exhaustion Cascade** (see [[10_System/Glossary/Mana Burn\|Mana Burn]]).

### Reserved Capacity (The RAM Rule)
Mana Capacity functions more like RAM than a gas tank. Any sustained spell, triggered ward, or un-discharged magical effect actively anchors itself in the caster's soul. It permanently reduces the caster's maximum Mana Capacity by its Mana Cost for as long as it exists in the world. 

You cannot take a "Partial Rest" to regenerate that lost Capacity while the spell is active—because it's not lost, it is actively "in use" holding the magic together.

**Recovery:** Because Capacity represents physical and spiritual mana capacity, it is recovered through resting. A partial rest (sitting quietly between classes) restores 3 Capacity. A full night's sleep restores all Capacity.
