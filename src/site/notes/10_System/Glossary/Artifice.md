# Artifice

**Artifice** (the Support magic discipline) is the specialized practice used in the creation of permanent Wards and magical items. It involves physically carving a [[50_Magic/Glossary/Spell Formula\|Spell Formula]] into an object to anchor its Root Words into a carefully-crafted diagram of geometric shapes and sigils, making them persist indefinitely. 

Artifice limits [[10_System/Glossary/mana\|mana]] flow along much tighter boundaries than standard [[10_System/Glossary/Shaping\|Shaping]], making the effect highly specialized but completely inflexible. Once etched, the Artifice is rigid; the spell **cannot be swapped, altered, or modulated**. Because the margin for error is so small, creating them requires high dexterity, mathematical expertise, and patience. 

Mechanically, mages use Artifice to prepare magic in advance. Inscribing a spell into an item provides three distinct mechanical trade-offs:
1. **Instant Activation:** Firing an Artifice ignores the caster's Calculation limit. It activates instantly—often as a reaction or free action.
2. **No Rolling Required:** Using an Artifice requires **no 2d6 casting roll** (it represents automatic baseline success).
3. **Double Mana Cost (The Built-in Limit):** Pushing raw mana passively through a physical object is wildly inefficient compared to bleeding it organically through live spiritual canals in a human soul. Firing an Artifice instantly causes **Double the normal Mana cost** for those spell roots. This ensures items cannot be spammed indefinitely without severe aura exhaustion.
